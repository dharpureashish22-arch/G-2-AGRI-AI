--- SECTION 1: SEED GENETICS (Drought-Resistant Wheat) ---
Target Crop: Triticum aestivum (Wheat)
Gene Modification Target: DREB1A (Dehydration-Responsive Element-Binding Protein 1A)
Editing Protocol: CRISPR-Cas9 system guided by sgRNA (Single Guide RNA).
Genetic Marker Sequence: ATGCGTAC-993-DREB
Action Mechanism: The edited DREB1A gene activates only when soil volumetric water content (VWC) drops below 15%. Upon activation, it alters the plant's stomatal closure rate, reducing transpiration by 42% without affecting photosynthesis.
Expected Yield: 3.5 tons per hectare even in severe drought conditions (temperatures up to 42°C).
CRISPR Cutting Site: Exon 2, position 452.

--- SECTION 2: SMART FERTILIZER (Slow-Release Nano-Urea) ---
Product Type: Polymeric Nano-Fertilizer (Nitrogen Base)
Core Material: Urea Nanoparticles (Size: 20-35 nanometers)
Coating Material: Cross-linked Chitosan-Polyacrylic acid (PAA) matrix.
Chemical Formula Core: CH4N2O (encapsulated)
Synthesis Process: Ionotropic gelation method at room temperature (25°C). 
Release Trigger Mechanism: 
1. Soil Moisture Threshold: > 25%
2. Soil pH Requirement: 6.5 to 7.2
Release Rate: Nitrogen is released at a controlled rate of 2.4 mg per day per gram of nano-urea. 
Efficiency: Reduces nitrogen leaching and volatilization by 60% compared to conventional granular urea. Dosage required is only 500ml per acre (mixed with 100 Liters of water for foliar spray).

--- SECTION 3: BIO-HERBICIDE (Targeted Weed Control) ---
Target Enzyme: EPSPS (5-enolpyruvylshikimate-3-phosphate synthase)
Target Weed: Amaranthus palmeri (Palmer Amaranth)
Active Molecule Base: A newly synthesized amino-acid analog.
SMILES Code for Active Molecule: C(C(=O)O)N(CP(=O)(O)O)C
Toxicity Profile: Highly specific to the EPSPS enzyme in broadleaf weeds. Non-toxic to mammals (LD50 > 5000 mg/kg).
Soil Half-life (DT50): 12 days. Rapid microbial degradation.
Application Protocol: Apply as a micro-emulsion. Requires addition of a non-ionic surfactant (0.25% v/v) to break the weed's leaf cuticle barrier.
Molecular Docking Note: The active molecule forms 3 stable hydrogen bonds with the Arg124, Pro105, and Ser115 residues of the weed's EPSPS protein, permanently blocking its metabolic pathway.
